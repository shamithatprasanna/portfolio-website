About Me

I'm currently pursuing my Master's in Computer Science at the University of Massachusetts, Amherst, with a specialization in Data Science. I'm driven by a passion for leveraging math-intensive algorithms and scalable engineering to build intelligent, technology-driven solutions — particularly at the intersection of software engineering and data science.

Before starting my Master's, I worked as a Full Stack Engineer at Fidelity Investments, where I began as an intern and earned a full-time role in the Corporate Services Technology business unit. Over the course of a year, I worked across the frontend and backend, integrating relational and non-relational databases, and gained exposure to cloud technologies like AWS, Jenkins, and Docker. I also led a team of five in an internal hackathon, building a Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) pipeline that cut vendor onboarding time from months to about a day.

My research background includes work in speech emotion recognition for early detection of depression, NLP-based test automation with Nokia R&D, and a multimodal speaker diarization framework with Samsung R&D — experiences that shaped my interest in combining machine learning with real-world, production-ready systems.

Going forward, I'm focused on deepening my expertise in building large-scale intelligent systems, with a long-term goal of developing accessible, data-driven healthcare solutions. I'm always looking to grow through dynamic projects and keep learning along the way.
